About Me and My Portfolio

My name is Jace Webster, and I am a PhD student at Washington University in St. Louis, in their Human and Statistical Genetics program. Before that, I was an undergraduate at BYU in Provo, Utah, where I studied Bioinformatics and Molecular Biology. Besides being a bit of a science nerd, I also enjoy playing soccer, reading, and spending time with my family.

This portfolio is a side project I have been developing, as a way to help me work on building new skills that aren't directly related to my formal academic training. While I get to do a fair amount of data crunching as a student in a statistical genetics program, genetics students don't often get to take classes in things like web development, web scraping, or natural language processing. So, when I find myself with a little spare time, I like to create projects designed to help me broaden my skillset. By design, these projects aren't necessarily exhaustive, but instead are meant to give me opportunities to try things I might not get to work on otherwise. While these projects are not meant to be tutorials, I do attempt to generally explain some of the logic and code behind them.

This portfolio, and the projects in it, are still growing, but feel free to check it out to see what's been done so far.
